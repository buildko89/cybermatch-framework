# CyberMatch Use Cases

## SOC / CSIRT

SOC and CSIRT teams can use CyberMatch to evaluate whether a defense strategy changes attacker behavior before critical impact.

Useful questions:

- Does the strategy delay critical path progress?
- Does it reduce attacker confidence or mission satisfaction?
- Does it increase observable events without relying on perfect detection?
- Does it remain effective under noise and adversarial signals?

Primary value:

CyberMatch turns response strategy into measurable campaign impact.

## Security Product Evaluation

Security teams can use CyberMatch to compare product classes and configurations using mission outcomes, not only alert counts.

Applicable product categories:

- IDS
- IPS
- Honeypot
- Deception platform
- SOAR
- XDR
- Mission-aware defense
- Counter-deception defense

Primary value:

CyberMatch can compare how products affect attacker utility, path choice, trust, exposure, and campaign completion.

## Purple Team Exercise

Purple teams can use CyberMatch before or after live exercises to reason about likely attacker adaptation.

Useful questions:

- Which path is the attacker likely to prefer?
- Which decoy or honey node placement creates the most waste?
- Which defense campaign changes attacker behavior fastest?
- Does an aware attacker recover after learning deception?

Primary value:

CyberMatch provides a repeatable decision model around exercises that are otherwise expensive and hard to reproduce.

## Security Training

CyberMatch can support training for defenders who need to think in campaigns rather than isolated alerts.

Training objectives:

- Understand mission-aware defense
- Understand deception and counter-deception
- Understand attacker belief and trust
- Understand coalition coordination limits
- Understand why detection alone is not enough

Primary value:

CyberMatch makes defender choices visible through attacker decision consequences.

## Research Platform

Researchers can use CyberMatch to study attacker-defender co-evolution without relying on RL or opaque agent training.

Research topics:

- Decision Neutralization
- Adaptive defense
- Mission belief inference
- Deception robustness
- Counter-deception
- Coalition coordination cost
- Awareness and hunting behavior

Primary value:

CyberMatch offers controlled, explainable, reproducible experiments for campaign-level cyber decision research.
